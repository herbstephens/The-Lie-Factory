## About the Book

**You've been faking it. Not just in bed—in life. Not just in relationships—in the mirror.**

Before you could walk, the adults you trusted most lied to you. They told you about Santa Claus, the Tooth Fairy, and the Easter Bunny. They sat you in front of Disney movies where love was instant, destined, and ended at the altar—and where women had impossibly perfect faces. They took you to church, where your body became evidence of sin.

Then they taught you to lie with your very appearance. Paint your face before anyone sees you. Fix what nature got wrong. Cover up who you actually are.

And they taught you lies about money. They called it "investing" when less than ten percent of it builds anything new. They called it "making your money work for you" when really it's other people working for you.

By the time you were old enough to want someone—really want them, with your whole body and your secret midnight thoughts—you had already been trained to lie about it. To yourself. To them. To everyone.

*The Lie Factory* traces a line that no one else has dared to draw: from the "harmless" myths of childhood directly into the adult bedroom, bathroom mirror, and brokerage account—where millions of people perform intimacy they don't feel, hide desires they can't speak, cover faces they've been taught to hate, and dress up extraction as virtue.

## About the Author

**Herb Stephens** was born in Flint, Michigan, the second of eight children in a Baptist household. He learned early that questioning was unwelcome—and did it anyway. At seventeen, he left home with a vow: no more lies.

That vow has guided his life. He raised his daughter without Santa, without fairy tales, without shame, and without teaching her that her face needed to be covered. The words "To thine own self be true" are tattooed on his arm, a permanent reminder of the commitment he made half a lifetime ago.

He is the co-founder of [Democracy Earth Foundation](https://democracy.earth) and creator of TIME Protocol, working to build transparent systems that don't require trust—because they're verifiable.
